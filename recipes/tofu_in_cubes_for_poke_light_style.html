Tofu in Cubes for Poke Light Style

Description

The tofu cubes are lightly marinated with soy sauce, ginger, and sesame for a fresh, clean flavor that shines in every bite. With a tender texture and delicate taste, they're perfect for a light poke bowl, adding plant-based protein without weighing you down.

Ingredients

  • Firm tofu → ~120 to 150 g per serving (½ of a 400 g block)
  • Soy sauce → 2 tbsp
  • Toasted sesame oil → 1 tsp (just for aroma)
  • Juice of ½ lime or lemon
  • Fresh ginger, grated → 1 tsp
  • (Optional) pinch of garlic powder or chili flakes

Steps

  1. Press the tofu → wrap it in a clean towel and place a weight on top for 15 to 20 min to remove excess water.
  2. Cut into cubes.
  3. Marinate → place the cubes in a bowl with soy sauce, sesame oil, lime juice, and ginger. Let it sit for 20 to 30 min (the longer, the more flavor).
  4. Cook:
    • Airfryer: 180 °C (355 °F) for 10 to 15 min, shaking halfway.
    • Non-stick pan: with or without a tiny drizzle of oil, sear until golden on all sides.
  5. The result: crispy on the outside, soft on the inside, lightly citrusy and aromatic.

Personal Notes

  • I cut the tofu in half horizontally and then into cubes. It was harder to sear in the pan, but it was perfect for poke and as a snack though.
  • I marinated it in a ziplock bag for about just 30 minutes — it was perfect to mix the sauce and season the tofu cubes.
  • The flavor was perfect and super light on the stomach.
